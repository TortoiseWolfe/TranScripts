## Boolean Search Operators in Action

### AND Operator
Requires both terms to appear.

**Example:** `"Software Engineer" AND Amazon`
- Results: Software engineers at Amazon
- Can click "People" to see all matching profiles
- Shows jobs, posts, and people matching criteria

### OR Operator
Shows results matching either term.

**Example:** `"Full Stack Developer" OR "Front-End Developer"`
- Results: Both types of developers
- Useful when you're open to multiple role types

**Combined example:** `("Full Stack Developer" OR "Front-End Developer") AND Amazon`
- Results: Either role type specifically at Amazon

### NOT Operator
Excludes specific terms.

**Example:** `"Tech Recruiter" NOT Agency`
- Results: Tech recruiters excluding agency recruiters
- Useful for finding in-house recruiters

### Quotes
Ensures exact phrase matching.

**Example:** `"Machine Learning Engineer"`
- Results: Exact job title only
- Without quotes, words could appear separately

---

## Handling Title Variations

**Challenge:** Job titles are written inconsistently:
- Full Stack / Full-Stack / Fullstack
- Front End / Front-End / Frontend

**Solution options:**
- Use OR to cover variations: `"Full Stack" OR "Full-Stack" OR "Fullstack"`
- Search multiple times with different variations
- Test what returns most relevant results

---

## Targeting Large Companies

For companies with thousands of employees:

1. **Focus on specific departments** rather than searching all employees
2. **Target recruiters first** - they're the first line of contact
3. **Find hiring leads** - people who might manage the role
4. **Filter by job function** when available

**Key question:** "Who is sourcing for these roles?"
- Answer: Recruiters. That's your first target.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **AND** narrows results (must have both terms)
2. **OR** expands results (either term works)
3. **NOT** excludes unwanted results
4. **Quotes** ensure exact phrase matching
5. Combine operators for complex searches
6. LinkedIn is an active search tool - invest time learning it
7. Boolean searching is how recruiters find candidates
8. Learning these techniques puts you ahead of passive job seekers

## The PEAR Framework: Who to Connect With

Think of the acronym **PEAR**:

**P - Peers**
Developers who are a step ahead of you or at other companies you're excited to work with.

**E - Employers**
Hiring managers, recruiters at your target companies. When you apply to a job, the person who posted it is often listed - follow that person.

**A - Alumni**
TechJoy graduates, university alumni, boot camp peers. You don't have to know them. Shared context builds trust fast.

**R - Relevant Communities**
Tech meetup organizers, Discord or Slack group members, open source contributors.

---

## The Personalization Formula

**Context + Relevance + Small Ask**

### Example
"Hi Kai, fellow TechJoy alum here. Saw your work on [specific project]. Would love to connect and follow your updates."

### Why It Works
- Shows you're not mass-messaging
- Creates immediate common ground
- Makes it easy to say yes

---

## Connection Request Best Practices

### Do's
- Reference something specific (their post, project, shared background)
- Keep it to 2-3 sentences
- Make the ask small ("would love to connect" vs "can you refer me")

### Don'ts
- Generic messages: "I'd like to add you to my professional network"
- Asking for referrals before building rapport
- Walls of text

---

## After They Accept: Nurturing the Connection

Once connected, don't let it go cold:
- Follow their posts
- React to and comment on their content
- Send a thank-you note for any help
- Share relevant articles or insights

**The goal:** Build genuine relationships, not transactional exchanges.

---

## Target Company Research Strategy

1. Search for the company on LinkedIn
2. Follow the company page
3. Click on "People" to see employees
4. Filter by role (recruiters, engineers, hiring managers)
5. Look for "Actively Hiring" badge
6. Connect with relevant people

### Pro Tips
- Look at profiles of people you admire
- Check their recommendations - those recommenders might work at companies that interest you
- You can send connection requests without a note when just building your network
- Once connected, you can often find their email for direct outreach

---

**70-85% of roles are filled through networking.**

### Employee Referral Programs
Most companies have referral bonuses ($200-$15,000). When your connections hear about open positions, they think of people they enjoyed working with.

### The Hidden Job Market
Many roles are never posted publicly. They're shared through internal referrals or recommendations.

### Referral Power
A referral gives you 4x higher chances of getting an interview.

People love to talk about themselves. Approach them with curiosity about:
- Their role and contributions
- The company culture
- Projects they're working on

Through this avenue, you build relationships naturally. Then opportunities flow to you.

### Don't Use the Banner

## Salary Negotiation Insights

### Internal Equity Matters

Recruiters consider internal equity - what current employees at similar levels make.

**Scenario:** If the highest person on team makes $150K and you want $160K, the company may not be able to offer that even if the budget allows it.

### Coming in at Top of Band

If you come in at the top of the salary band, you won't get raises the next year because you've already hit the ceiling.

**Ideal:** Come in with room to grow, not hitting a glass ceiling.

### The Bigger Picture

Questions to consider:
- Is this the opportunity you want?
- Is this the culture you want?
- Will this help you reach your personal goals?

"Sometimes it's about the opportunity. Sometimes you have to go back $10K."

But also: "Don't go to work with jerks."

## Setting Up Job Alerts Step by Step

### 1. Navigate to Jobs
Click the briefcase icon (Jobs) in the top navigation.

### 2. Enter Your Target Role
In the "Describe the job you want" field, type your target job title.
- Just the role, not location yet
- Example: "Full Stack Developer" or "QA Engineer"

### 3. Set Date Filter
Click "Date posted" and select **Past 24 hours**.
- This filters to only the freshest postings
- Jobs posted 4 weeks ago = too late

### 4. Turn On Job Alert
Look for "Job search alert is on" toggle.
- Turn this ON for searches you want alerts for
- LinkedIn will email you when matching jobs are posted

### 5. Set Location (Optional)
Add location filters as needed (city, remote, etc.)

## Why Messaging Matters in 2025

### 1. Referrals Beat Applications
The hidden job market is alive and well. Most interviews don't start with an online application - they start with a person.

**A warm intro from:**
- An alum
- A recruiter remembering your message

**Key insight:** A referral can move your application from a pile of 500 to the top 10 considered in under 24 hours.

### 2. Short Supply of Attention
Hiring managers, recruiters, and alumni are busy. They often skim LinkedIn on their phone between meetings.

- Long messages get skipped
- Short, clear, specific messages get replies
- Best outreach takes 15-20 seconds to read

**Signal immediately:** Who you are, why you're reaching out, what you're asking for.

### 3. AI Filters Everywhere
In 2025, most recruiters and hiring platforms use AI to sort, scan, and flag messages.

- Generic copy-paste templates get caught in filters, deprioritized, or ignored

## What is a Boolean Search?

A search method using operators (AND, OR, NOT, quotes, parentheses) to refine results.

**Why it matters:**
- Recruiters use boolean searching every day to find talent
- Job seekers can use it to research recruiters, companies, and target roles
- It's a logic-based way of telling LinkedIn exactly what to include or exclude

---

## Boolean Search Operators

### AND (Must include both terms)
`"Software Engineer" AND Amazon`
- Shows only software engineers who work at Amazon

### OR (Include either term)
`"Full Stack Developer" OR "Front-End Developer"`
- Shows both types of developers

### NOT (Exclude a term)
`Recruiter NOT HR`
- Shows recruiters but excludes HR recruiters

### Quotes (Exact phrase match)
`"Machine Learning Engineer"`
- Returns exact job title, not words separately

### Parentheses (Group terms for complex searches)
`("Full Stack Developer" OR "Software Engineer") AND Amazon`
- Combines multiple conditions

---

## Boolean Search Examples

**Finding recruiters at a target company:**
```
"Technical Recruiter" AND Google
```

**Finding developers with specific skills:**
```
"Software Engineer" AND Amazon
```

**Finding multiple role types at a company:**
```
("Full Stack Developer" OR "Front-End Developer") AND Amazon
```

**Excluding certain recruiter types:**
```
"Tech Recruiter" NOT Agency
```

2. Check recent growth and hiring trends
3. Find recruiters using boolean search
4. Identify people in your target role
5. Connect strategically with personalized notes

## Words to Avoid in Your Summary

Based on conversations with recruiters, chief talent officers, HR directors, and talent acquisition leaders across advertising, tech startups, and technology companies:

**Avoid:**
- "Seasoned"
- "Junior"
- "Aspiring"
- Years of experience (e.g., "25+ years")

Who you are + what you do + what you're working toward.
