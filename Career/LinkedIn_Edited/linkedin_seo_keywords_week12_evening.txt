## Why SEO Matters for LinkedIn

Recruiters search LinkedIn the same way they search Google: by using keywords.

**The right keywords:** Increase your chances of appearing in recruiter searches

**Wrong or missing keywords:** Make your profile invisible, even if you're highly qualified

### Key Statistics
- **90% of recruiters** use keyword filters to find candidates
- Candidates with keyword-optimized profiles receive **2-3x more views** from recruiters

### Goals
1. Match recruiter search terms
2. Rank high in search results
3. Attract more recruiter messages and interview opportunities

---

## What is LinkedIn Recruiter?

A separate paid platform that recruiters use to search for candidates. It's different from regular LinkedIn but part of the same ecosystem.

Recruiters use it to:
- Run advanced boolean searches
- Filter by location, experience, skills
- Track and manage candidate pipelines
- Reach out to potential hires

## Key Fields That Impact LinkedIn SEO

### Headline
Weighted heavily by LinkedIn's algorithm. Don't just put "Software Developer" - add multiple keywords for maximum visibility.

### About Section
Scanned for relevant skills and job titles.

### Experience Section
Job titles, skills, and project descriptions are indexed.

### Skills Section
Top 50 skills are searchable and contribute to ranking.

### Recommendations
Keywords in recommendations also count.

### Engagement
Posts and comments including relevant terms improve visibility.

## How Recruiters Search

Recruiters enter boolean searches and filter by:
- Location
- Industry
- Experience level
- Keywords

Profiles are ranked by keyword relevance and engagement.

### Example Boolean Search
```
("Full Stack Developer" OR "Software Engineer") AND (React OR NodeJS OR JavaScript) AND (API OR PostgreSQL OR AWS)
```

**What this tells LinkedIn:** Find developers who know React, NodeJS, or JavaScript AND have experience with APIs, PostgreSQL, or AWS.

---

## Mirroring: The Key Concept

LinkedIn matches words in recruiter searches to words in your profile. If you use different terms or omit keywords, you won't appear in results.

**You must use the same words recruiters use, not just words you prefer.**

### How to Know What Recruiters Search
They use the same professional terms from job descriptions. Pull 3-5 job postings for your target role and identify repeating keywords.

## How to Mirror Recruiter Searches

### Step 1: Analyze Target Job Postings
- Review 3-5 postings of your dream role
- Highlight repeating terms
- Note job title variations (Software Engineer, Full Stack Developer, Front-End Engineer)

### Step 2: Embed Keywords Across Profile
- **Headline:** Target title + top 3 skills
- **About:** Phrases recruiters type
- **Experience:** Tie each project to target keywords
- **Skills:** Every hard skill from multiple postings

### Step 3: Match Variations
Use multiple forms:
- API and REST API
- ReactJS and React
- AWS and Amazon Web Services
- SQL and PostgreSQL

---

## Avoid Keyword Stuffing

**Don't:** Cram a list of terms without context

**Weak example:** React, NodeJS, PostgreSQL, AWS, API, Agile

**Strong example:** Developed full stack applications using React and NodeJS. Managed PostgreSQL databases and deployed to AWS using CI/CD pipelines.

Always use keywords in real sentences showing how you apply the skill.

---

## LinkedIn Favors Active Profiles

- Post at least once a week using relevant keywords
- Example: "Excited to finish my React app today"
- Comment on posts using industry language
- Follow companies and hashtags reflecting target roles

---

## Managing Extensive Experience

If you have 10+ years of experience with many skills:
- Focus on **current** skills that are in demand
- Bundle similar skills together
- Highlight how past experience connects to current goals
- You don't need to list everything - prioritize relevance

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Recruiters search by keywords - optimize or be invisible
2. 90% of recruiters use keyword filters
3. Mirror the exact language from job postings
4. Keywords must appear in headline, about, experience, and skills
5. Avoid keyword stuffing - always provide context
6. Include title variations
7. Active engagement boosts search ranking
8. Take profile updates section by section - don't get overwhelmed
