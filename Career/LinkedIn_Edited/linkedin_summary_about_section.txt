## What is the Summary Section?

The summary appears just under your headline, name, contact info, GitHub, and portfolio links - right underneath your banner.

**Key characteristics:**
- One of the few narrative parts of LinkedIn
- Shows up in search results
- Gives a voice to your resume
- It's the first step recruiters take when researching who you are

**LinkedIn vs Resume:**
- LinkedIn = narrative part of who you are (human approach)
- Resume = more formal document, legacy of your work history

---

## Core Elements of a Strong Summary

1. **Clear and confident opening line**
2. **A sense of who you are and what you're about professionally**
3. **What you're working on now and looking for**
4. **A sentence or two reflecting your personality, values, motivation**
5. **Call to action (optional)** - open to networking, hiring, or collaboration

---

## Words to Avoid in Your Summary

Based on conversations with recruiters, chief talent officers, HR directors, and talent acquisition leaders across advertising, tech startups, and technology companies:

**Avoid:**
- "Seasoned"
- "Junior"
- "Aspiring"
- Years of experience (e.g., "25+ years")

**Why:** Recruiters can determine your level from your resume and LinkedIn profile. Keep things ambiguous in that regard. The details about years of experience are for conversations in initial phone screens.

## Best Practices for LinkedIn Summary

### 1. Open with a Strong, Concise Sentence

Who you are + what you do + what you're working toward.

**Example:**
"I am a front-end developer with a background in visual design and a passion for building intuitive user experiences."

### 2. Use Plain, Confident Language

- Avoid buzzwords and filler
- Write like a human who understands their work, not a press release
- Words like "results-driven" and "team-oriented" are overused - find unique ways to express these qualities

### 3. Highlight What You Build or Solve

- Mention technologies you use
- Problems you love solving
- Recent projects
- Focus on outcomes, not just tasks
- Include metrics or percentages when possible

### 4. Add a Personal/Human Touch

A line about:
- How you think
- What drives you
- Why tech excites you

This makes your summary memorable and real.

## Summary Structure Framework

**Line 1:** Who you are and what you're currently doing
- "I am [job title/role] with a background in [relevant area] and a focus on [skill or goal]."

**Lines 2-4:** Your work, skills, values, current learning journey

**Line 5 (optional):** What you're open to or excited about

## Example Summary: Career Changer (Architecture to Tech)

"I'm a front-end developer with a background in architecture and a growing passion for building responsive, accessible web experiences. I specialize in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and recently completed a React-focused boot camp where I built a task manager app with real-time form validation.

Before transitioning into tech, I managed cross-disciplinary design projects and collaborated with engineers, clients, and city agencies, which taught me how to communicate clearly and think in systems.

I am currently diving deeper into TypeScript, accessibility best practices, and test-driven development. I love clean design, collaborative code, and solving the kind of problems that make life a little easier for users."

## About the "Junior" Title

**Context:** TechJoy Academy graduates as "Junior Developer" - that's the official title from the program.

**Guidance:**
- In the **summary section** (LinkedIn and resume): No need to say "I am a junior developer"
- In the **experience section**: If your official title was "Junior Software Developer," list it accurately
- Recruiters can see your level from your experience section
- Titles vary tremendously from organization to organization

**Why this matters:** You might interview and speak like a senior even if your title is junior. Companies may place you in a role without "junior" in the title. The summary is for selling yourself and showing where you want to go.

---

## Handling Multiple Career Interests

**Question:** What if you have conflicting roles you want to pursue (e.g., game producer vs training manager)?

**Answer:** Your LinkedIn summary can cover both if the skills overlap. Example:
- "I am a full stack developer with a passion for gaming and operations"
- Cast a wide net - you're supposed to in the summary
- The goal is to have options and get noticed by as many people as possible

**Key insight:** Your LinkedIn profile shows you as a human being. Casting a wide net is acceptable in the summary as long as there's some level of specificity.

## Q&A Highlights

**Q: If we remove "junior" from everywhere, we're basically saying "developer" right off the bat?**
A: We're talking specifically about the summary section. List your actual title in the experience section. The summary is about selling yourself and showing progression.

**Q: Should I mention how long I've been doing something?**
A: Avoid putting years. That's for the phone screen conversation with recruiters.

**Q: Can I use AI to help write my summary?**
A: Yes, but be very descriptive in your prompts. List words you don't want (seasoned, detail-oriented), specify the tone you want (professional but warm), and include relevant background to incorporate.
