### The Six-Second Scan (Review)

When a recruiter opens your resume, they spend **6 to 10 seconds** on the first scan. They look for:

- **The top third** of your resume
- **Job titles and company names**
- **Visible metrics or measurable outcomes**
- **Keywords that match the role**

What makes a recruiter stop scrolling:

- A **clear target role or headline** at the top
- **Bullets that show outcomes, not tasks**
- **Consistency** between your resume and LinkedIn profile

## [12:34](https://youtu.be/odPIvVCLsYs?t=754) The Metrics Problem for Early-Career Developers

Chelsea spoke with **tech recruiter colleagues** about the concern that TechJoy students can't quantify their project impact. The key finding:

> **Your resume will NOT look unbelievable and will NOT prevent you from getting an interview if you don't list measurable results.**

If you don't list fabricated metrics, it will actually make you look **authentic** — as long as you focus on:

- **Evidence of process**
- **Scope**
- **As much outcome as you know** (not hard business metrics)

Recruiters understand that **early-career developers** and pre-production contributors often don't have production data.

## [17:25](https://youtu.be/odPIvVCLsYs?t=1045) Impact Logic: Six Strategies for Credible Bullets

Tech recruiters recommended writing with **impact logic** — credible bullets when you can't quantify numbers.

### 1. Show Scope Instead of Data

- "Developed authentication flow for full-stack MERN app with **JWT and React Context**"
- "Built **6 REST API endpoints** to handle CRUD operations for product data"

This scope language shows **technical depth** without requiring user metrics.

### 2. Show Quality or Improvement Language

- "Improved app reliability by **refactoring frontend components** for reusable logic"
- "Enhanced code readability and **reduced redundancy** through modularization"

Recruiters recognize these as **measurable improvements even without percentages**.

### 3. Use Frequency or Scale When Exact Numbers Aren't Tracked

- "Debugged **multiple front-end issues weekly** in collaboration with QA team"
- "Implemented feature updates across **three sprint cycles** following agile methodology"

### 4. Emphasize Collaboration and Delivery

Show results in terms of **teamwork and contribution** rather than solo metrics.

### 5. Show Results in Terms of Learning or Contribution

Frame your work around **what you gained** and **what you delivered** to the project.

### 6. Use Project or Course Outcomes

**Boot camp or personal project outcomes still count.** Examples:

- "Completed **12-week coding boot camp** focused on full-stack JavaScript development"
- "Practiced version control, deployment, and testing in **real-time collaborative projects**"
- "Presented final capstone project to technical mentors and peers, explaining **architecture and decisions**"

**Optional soft skill bullet:** "Balanced multiple project deadlines while maintaining **code quality and documentation standards**"

## [23:22](https://youtu.be/odPIvVCLsYs?t=1402) The Bottom Line on Metrics

> **Recruiters read outcome as proof of ownership, not proof of revenue.**

You demonstrate credibility through **clarity of contribution**. The key is showing what you did, how you did it, and what it enabled.

## [25:30](https://youtu.be/odPIvVCLsYs?t=1530) Adapting Bullets to Your Resume

When rewriting your bullets:

- **Swap in your own project names** (task tracker app, weather dashboard, ecommerce API)
- **Use your tech stack language** (React, Vue, Flask, etc.)
- **Replace broad verbs** like "built" with **context verbs**: implemented, integrated, refactored, deployed
- **Keep every bullet under two lines** — concise, scannable, and results-oriented

## [27:24](https://youtu.be/odPIvVCLsYs?t=1644) Your Resume Reflects Your Personal Brand

Your resume should **sound like you** and reflect your professional brand. Start by cleaning up the **most critical area — the top third**:

- **Name**, city, email, phone, LinkedIn URL, GitHub, and/or portfolio (if relevant)
- **Target role line** (your headline): e.g., "Full Stack Developer | React | Node | API Integration"
- **Three to four line summary** that mirrors your LinkedIn about section

**Important:** Don't include irrelevant links. If you have a side hustle unrelated to your target role, don't put that website on this resume.

## [29:30](https://youtu.be/odPIvVCLsYs?t=1770) Live Bullet Rewrite Exercise

**Before:** "Led development of admin data management feature to improve relevance and timeliness of displayed data."

**After:** "Developed React admin dashboard integrated with **AWS S3** to provide full data visibility and manipulation."

The rewritten bullet uses the **Action + Task + Result formula** and includes specific technologies without relying on unverifiable metrics.

## [43:49](https://youtu.be/odPIvVCLsYs?t=2629) Target Role Headline Exercise

## [48:44](https://youtu.be/odPIvVCLsYs?t=2924) Brand Word Integration

Think of a couple of words that describe you — these are your **brand words**. Make sure they show up in your bullets and summary.

| Brand Word | Example Bullet |
|---|---|
| **Organized** | "Streamlined project workflow to reduce task overlap" |
| **Technical** | "Developed REST API integrations improving data flow accuracy" |

## [51:12](https://youtu.be/odPIvVCLsYs?t=3072) Skills Section Placement and ATS

**Skills should be listed at the top, underneath your summary.** This is because companies large and small are utilizing **AI within their applicant tracking systems** to parse resumes with keywords.

Having your skills prominently placed ensures that ATS systems with built-in AI features can highlight your resume for recruiters.
