## Michelle: Avoiding Ageism on Your Resume

Chelsea flagged a common mistake: listing specific years of experience (e.g., "20+ years" or "25+ years"). In a society where ageism is real, this can work against you. Also consider removing graduation dates from the education section.

### Words to Avoid

- **"Seasoned"** -- vague, overused, and can imply "old" rather than "experienced"

### Alternative Phrases for Deep Experience

- Tenured
- Established
- **Extensive experience in [field]**
- Highly skilled
- Proficient
- Advanced
- Highly competent
- **Strong background in [field]**

### Words That Convey Reliability

- Proven
- Trusted
- Consistent
- Reliable
- **Track record**

### Words for Practical Experience

- **Hands-on**
- Practical

### Recruiter-Friendly Tone Alternatives

- Experience
- **Professional background spanning [field/industry]**
- Brings experience in [area]
- Experience across [domains]

The goal is to highlight what you bring to the table and why your depth of experience makes you the most qualified candidate -- without telegraphing age.

## David: Resume Review

### Hyperlinks for Projects

- **Add hyperlinks directly to project names** in the experience section (e.g., link "Support Local" to the live project URL). David had a link further down but Chelsea recommended putting it right on the project name where recruiters first encounter it.

### TechJoy Naming

- The experience section header should reference **TechJoy** (or TechJoy Academy), not just the project name "Support Local." TechJoy is the organization; Support Local is one project within it.

### Project Ordering

- Chelsea suggested reordering projects so the most relevant ones appear first under the experience section.

### Customer Service Section

- Chelsea recommended **removing the customer service and operations section** to avoid confusing recruiters. When your target role is in tech, a lengthy non-tech section can dilute the message.
- David's concern: he wanted employers to see he is currently working and not idle. Chelsea acknowledged this is a valid concern but noted recruiters generally do not assume someone is doing nothing -- and the section creates confusion about career direction.
- Decision was left to David to finalize by next week.

## ATS Tip: Remove Decorative Lines

- **Decorative horizontal lines in your resume can interfere with Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS).** Chelsea confirmed she is removing them from her own templates as well. Even though they look nice visually, the parsing technology can misread them.

---

## Google Docs vs. Microsoft Word

- Chelsea recommended **editing resumes in Google Docs** rather than Microsoft Word. Google Docs is easier to edit without accidentally breaking formatting.
- Google Drive also makes it easy to **keep multiple resume versions in one place** -- useful if you have different target roles or industries. You can maintain a master template, customize per application, and save each submitted version for reference.
