## [8:01](https://youtu.be/SnPgMS1FkqQ?t=481) Session Goal: Tailor Summaries to Real Jobs

This session builds on the Week 3 summary structure. The goal is to **refine summaries** and learn how to **tailor them to real job descriptions** in the current market.

**Recap of the 4-line summary structure:**
- **Line 1:** Who you are and your main focus
- **Line 2:** What you do well, including tools or skills
- **Line 3:** Proof of impact or contribution
- **Line 4/5:** What you want next and how you want to add value

---

## [15:25](https://youtu.be/SnPgMS1FkqQ?t=925) Live Demo: Tailoring a Summary with ChatGPT

**The process for tailoring a summary to a specific job:**

1. Open ChatGPT (or Claude or any AI platform)
2. Provide context: "I am a front-end developer at the final stages of completing a coding boot camp. I am ready to apply for open roles on LinkedIn and need help with my resume summary."
3. Paste your current resume summary
4. Say: "Tailor the provided resume summary to this job" and **paste the full job description** (links alone may not work -- copy and paste the entire description)
5. Review the generated options

**Key tip:** The more information and context you supply, the better the outcome.

## [34:30](https://youtu.be/SnPgMS1FkqQ?t=2070) Handling AI Detection Concerns

Some recruiters claim they will not look at AI-generated resumes. Practical advice:

- **Remove the em dash** (--) and replace with commas wherever it appears -- this is a common AI signal
- Tell ChatGPT: **"Do not make it sound like it was generated from AI. Make it sound human."**
- If that does not work, take the output and **change it yourself** -- give it your own voice and flair
- If the summary hits on all the points that are true about your skill set and experience, a recruiter reading it **will not think it was AI-generated**
- AI-generated content that is truthful and well-edited is still a legitimate tool

## [42:22](https://youtu.be/SnPgMS1FkqQ?t=2542) Generic vs Tailored Summary

**Generic summary:**
> Full-stack developer with experience in JavaScript and React. Builds full-stack applications and enjoys learning new technologies. Seeking software development roles.

**Tailored summary:**
> Full-stack developer with experience in JavaScript, React, Node.js, and REST APIs. Builds and tests features that integrate with third-party services and back-end systems. Completed projects that include authentication, API integration, and responsive UI. Targeting roles where I can contribute to feature development and automation testing within an agile team.

The tailored version pulls in specific keywords, tools, and requirements from the target job description.

## [44:12](https://youtu.be/SnPgMS1FkqQ?t=2652) Checking Consistency Across Your Assets

When you finalize a summary, check for consistency:

- Your resume summary should **match your LinkedIn headline and about section in tone** (not identical, but mirroring each other)
- Your **job titles and skills** in the experience section should match the kinds of roles you actually apply for
- Look at the roles you are applying for, find consistency across them, and start curating your headline to include skills required in those job descriptions

---

## [47:35](https://youtu.be/SnPgMS1FkqQ?t=2855) Common Consistency Mistakes

- If your resume summary targets **full-stack roles** but your LinkedIn headline says "open to anything" or just "developer" -- that **confuses recruiters**
- If your summary lists **Python and SQL** but your skills section does not include them -- that **looks careless**

## [48:12](https://youtu.be/SnPgMS1FkqQ?t=2892) Action Steps for LinkedIn and Resume Alignment

1. **Copy your final summary** into your LinkedIn about section and adapt it there
2. In ChatGPT, say: "This is my resume summary. Create a new summary for my LinkedIn about section. This new LinkedIn about summary should mirror my resume summary **in tone only**. It should not be identical."
3. **Check your target role** is the same across your resume and LinkedIn profile
4. **Check that your top 3 skills** appear in your summary, skills section, LinkedIn about, and LinkedIn skills

This positions you as someone who understands **brand consistency** and recruiter workflow.

## [50:59](https://youtu.be/SnPgMS1FkqQ?t=3059) You Are a Brand

Your LinkedIn profile is your **storefront** -- how you market yourself. Your resume is **proof**.

That is why they need to be a little different from each other. LinkedIn is how you advertise and market who you are. The resume is the tangible document that backs it up.

## [53:26](https://youtu.be/SnPgMS1FkqQ?t=3206) Self-Assessment Checklist

Before finalizing your summary, check:

- [ ] Does my summary state a **clear target role**?
- [ ] Does it list **key tools or skills** that match real job posts?
- [ ] Does it include at least **one piece of proof**?
- [ ] Does it say what I **want to do next**?
- [ ] Can someone **skim it in 5 seconds** and understand my direction?

If you answered yes to most of these, you are in strong shape.

## [54:50](https://youtu.be/SnPgMS1FkqQ?t=3290) Bonus: LinkedIn Projects Section

LinkedIn has a **hidden "Projects" section** under Add Profile Section > Recommended > Add Projects.

This lets you list projects with descriptions and links. Useful for career changers who want to show coding work (scripts, games, tools) from throughout their career that may not fit neatly into job experience. The proof is right there for recruiters to see.
